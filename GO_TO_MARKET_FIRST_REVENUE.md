# WrenchPro Go-To-Market First Revenue Plan

Date: 2026-04-29  
Status: Internal plan; no outreach or public claims approved yet.

## Executive Decision

WrenchPro should not try to make money through a broad public launch first. The fastest credible path to revenue is a controlled **Founding Mechanic beta** after the product passes basic revenue-readiness checks.

The reason: WrenchPro is already feature-rich, but money will come from trust. A mechanic will pay only if the app safely handles the daily money workflow: lead/customer/vehicle -> estimate -> job -> invoice/payment -> warranty/follow-up.

## First Revenue Offer

### Offer Name

**WrenchPro Founding Mechanic Package**

### Target Buyer

Solo mobile mechanics and very small mobile mechanic businesses that currently run jobs through texts, calls, paper, spreadsheets, and generic invoices.

### Core Promise

A practical Windows desktop command center for mobile mechanics: customers, vehicles, leads, estimates, jobs, parts, payments, warranties, and follow-ups in one local-first system without expensive shop software.

### Initial Pricing Recommendation

Use a two-step pricing path:

1. **First controlled paid test:** $149 one-time founding license.
   - Best for the very first paid user because it reduces friction and proves willingness to pay.
2. **After workflow/support confidence:** $29/month or $299/year.
   - Move to $49/month or $499/year only after installer, backup, docs, support, and workflow reliability are strong.

This matches competitor research: mature shop SaaS costs far more, but WrenchPro should not price like mature SaaS before reliability and support are proven.

## Why This Path Makes Money

1. **Clear pain:** mobile mechanics lose time and money when jobs, estimates, customers, payments, and follow-ups are scattered.
2. **Low-friction buyer:** solo mechanics can make purchasing decisions without committees.
3. **Focused wedge:** WrenchPro does not need to beat Shopmonkey or Tekmetric; it needs to be simpler and cheaper for small mobile operators.
4. **Controlled support:** starting with 1-3 mechanics prevents support chaos.
5. **Payment signal:** even one paid founding mechanic proves more than many free testers.
6. **Upsell path:** once useful, WrenchPro can add paid support/updates, subscription, setup help, reminders, backups, and eventually cloud/mobile features.

## Funnel

### Stage 1 — Internal Proof

Audience: Brandon + brother.

Goal:
- prove the workflow on real/simulated mobile mechanic jobs.

Exit criteria:
- installed app launches
- data persists
- lead/customer/vehicle/estimate/job/payment workflow works
- invoice/receipt output is usable
- backup/update behavior is documented
- known limitations are honest

### Stage 2 — Free Controlled Beta

Audience: 1-3 trusted mechanics approved by Brandon.

Duration: 14-30 days.

Goal:
- prove the workflow generalizes beyond one user.

Agent responsibilities:
- prepare invite copy
- prepare onboarding docs
- prepare feedback forms/templates
- triage feedback
- identify blockers

Brandon approval required before contacting anyone.

### Stage 3 — Founding Paid Conversion

Offer after the trial:

> Continue with WrenchPro as a founding mechanic for $149. You get the local desktop app, beta updates for 12 months, and basic support while helping shape the product.

Success target:
- Minimum: 1 paying mechanic.
- Strong signal: 2 of 3 testers pay or strongly request continued use.

### Stage 4 — Early Commercial Package

Only after beta evidence:

- $29/month or $299/year for solo/small mobile mechanics.
- Later $49/month or $499/year when support/docs/backups are stronger.
- Consider $399-$699 one-time desktop license for customers who strongly dislike subscriptions.
