## Ideal Job Lifecycle

A normal job likely moves through these stages:

1. Lead / first contact
2. Customer created or found
3. Vehicle created or found
4. Problem/service requested
5. Estimate created
6. Customer approves estimate
7. Job scheduled
8. Parts ordered / inventory checked
9. Work performed
10. Inspection/results documented
11. Invoice/payment collected
12. Warranty/follow-up/reminder created
13. Job closed

## Questions For Brandon / Business Owner

### Customers

- What customer info is required?
- Do customers have multiple vehicles?
- Does the business need customer notes/tags/status?
- Are commercial/fleet customers possible?

### Vehicles

- What vehicle info matters most?
- VIN required or optional?
- Mileage required?
- License plate/state needed?
- Should service history be vehicle-based?

### Jobs

- What job statuses are needed?
- What is the normal job flow?
- Does every job require an estimate first?
- Can a job have multiple services?
- Should jobs track parts, labor, fees, taxes, discounts?

### Estimates / Invoices

- Does he need printed/PDF estimates?
- Does he need printed/PDF invoices?
- Should estimates convert into jobs/invoices?
- What payment methods are accepted?
- Does he collect deposits?

### Scheduling

- Does he schedule by day, exact time, or route/location?
- Does travel time matter?
- Does mileage/travel fee matter?
- Does he need reminders for appointments?

### Parts / Inventory

- Does he maintain inventory or buy parts per job?
- Does he need vendor/source tracking?
- Does he need part cost vs customer price?
- Should low-stock alerts exist?

### Inspections

- Does he use inspection checklists?
- Are photos needed?
- Does he need pass/fail/safety notes?

### Warranty / Follow-Up

- Does he offer warranties?
- Warranty by service, part, date, or mileage?
- Does he need automatic reminders?

### Reports

- What does he need to know weekly/monthly?
- Revenue?
- Expenses?
- Profit per job?
- Open estimates?
- Unpaid invoices?
- Tax reports?
